# SpaceBio Twin

SpaceBio Twin is an AI-powered digital twin designed to simulate the biological effects of long-duration spaceflight. Inspired by NASA space biology research, the platform models how mission conditions such as radiation exposure, mission duration, shielding, and exercise influence key biological systems.

The project aims to bridge predictive simulation and computational intervention discovery by generating both biological risk assessments and potential countermeasure hypotheses.

## Project Vision

Future deep-space missions to the Moon and Mars will expose astronauts to unique environmental stressors, including cosmic radiation, microgravity, and prolonged isolation. These conditions can affect critical biological processes such as:

- DNA damage and repair
- Immune system function
- Mitochondrial health
- Inflammatory signaling
- Cellular aging and senescence

SpaceBio Twin provides a computational framework for exploring these risks and evaluating possible mitigation strategies.

## Current Features

### Mission Simulator

Users can configure mission parameters including:

- Mission duration
- Radiation exposure level
- Shielding effectiveness
- Exercise compliance

The simulator generates predicted risk scores across multiple biological domains.

### Biological Risk Dashboard

Current biological outputs include:

- DNA Damage
- Mitochondrial Stress
- Immune Dysfunction
- Inflammation
- Cellular Senescence

### Countermeasure Engine

Based on predicted biological disruptions, SpaceBio Twin generates hypothesis-driven countermeasure suggestions, including:

- Shielding modifications
- Exercise interventions
- Pathway-targeted mitigation strategies
- Candidate therapeutic exploration directions
